GREEK PORK SOUVLAKI

Pork Souvlaki

Greek pork souvlaki is a mouthwatering dish that captures the essence of Greek cuisine. Tender pieces of marinated pork are skewered and grilled to perfection, creating a succulent and flavorful treat. The marinade typically consists of a harmonious blend of olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and aromatic herbs like oregano and thyme. This combination infuses the pork with a delightful Mediterranean flavor that is both tangy and fragrant. The grilled chicken is traditionally served on a warm and fluffy pita bread, accompanied by a variety of fresh and vibrant toppings. Common additions include crisp lettuce, juicy tomatoes, sliced onions, and creamy tzatziki sauce. The tzatziki, made with yogurt, cucumber, garlic, and dill, adds a cool and refreshing element that complements the smoky chicken perfectly. Greek pork souvlaki is a beloved street food in Greece and has gained popularity worldwide for its irresistible taste. With its tender and juicy pork, aromatic marinade, and a medley of fresh toppings, each bite is a delectable explosion of Mediterranean flavors. Whether enjoyed as a quick snack or a satisfying meal, Greek pork souvlaki is a culinary delight that transports you to the sun-kissed shores of Greece.

Ingrediënts:

  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 1 medium lemon, juiced
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 3 pounds pork tenderloin, cut into 1 inch cubes
  • 2 medium yellow onions, cut into 1 inch pieces
  • 2 medium green bell peppers, cut into 1 inch pieces
  • skewers

Steps:

  1. Mix oil, soy sauce, lemon juice, garlic, and oregano, and garlic together in a large glass bowl. Add pork, onions, and bell peppers, and stir until coated. Cover and refrigerate for 2 to 3 hours.
  2. Preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  3. Remove pork and vegetables from marinade and shake off excess; thread onto skewers. Discard remaining marinade.
  4. Cook on the preheated grill, turning frequently, until pork is no longer pink in the middle, 10 to 15 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 145 degrees F (63 degrees C).
